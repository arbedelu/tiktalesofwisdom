Traditional Filipino Proverbs

Traditional Filipino proverbs (salawikain) are expressions of cultural values and beliefs passed down through generations, reflecting Filipino worldview and social norms. They serve as moral guides and are still used in daily life and advice-giving (Payung & Supaporn, 2023; Matammu, 2022).

Digitalized Filipino Proverbs

In modern contexts, proverbs evolve through digital platforms where they are adapted into informal, creative forms (e.g., memes or posts) while keeping their original meaning. Research shows that proverbs continue to change and circulate in digital spaces like social media, reflecting contemporary culture (Davis et al., 2021).

The Proverbs

"Habang may buhay, may pag-asa"

Traditional

As long as a person is alive, there is still hope. It teaches resilience and reminds people not to give up even during difficult times.

Digitalized

The new version, "Habang may buhay, may paasa," shifts the meaning from hope to false hope — warning people to be careful of those who give empty promises or lead others on.

"pag-asa" → "paasa" — hope becomes heartbreak.

"Nasa Diyos ang awa, nasa tao ang gawa"

Traditional

God may show mercy and guidance, but people must still work hard and act. Success does not come from prayer alone; human effort is also necessary.

Digitalized

The adapted version, "Nasa Diyos ang awa, nasa babae ang tama," turns the serious message into humor — joking that women are usually right, especially in arguments or relationships.

"nasa tao ang gawa" → "nasa babae ang tama" — faith becomes a punchline.

"Kung kaya ng iba, kaya mo rin"

Traditional

If other people can succeed through effort and determination, then you can also achieve your goals. It encourages confidence, perseverance, and self-belief.

Digitalized

The adapted version, "Kung kaya ng iba, ipagawa mo sa kanila," turns it into a joke about avoiding stress — humorously suggesting you let more capable people do the work instead.

Motivation becomes a stress-free, lazy-day anthem.

"Kapag may tiyaga, may nilaga"

Traditional

Patience and perseverance lead to rewards. Nilaga is presented as a reward after hard work, showing that success comes to those who work patiently and consistently.

Digitalized

The new meaning applies the proverb to love — suggesting that if you are patient with someone you like, you may eventually be rewarded by having that person become yours.

Work ethic becomes romantic patience.

"Huwag mong gawin sa iba ang ayaw mong gawin nila sa iyo"

Traditional

Do not do something bad to others if you do not want them to do the same to you. It teaches fairness, respect, and empathy.

Digitalized

The new meaning connects the proverb to love — you should not expect good treatment, loyalty, or kindness in a relationship if you cannot also give those things to others.

The lesson stayed similar, placed in a romantic context.

"Kapag may usok, may apoy"

Traditional

Where there is smoke, there is fire. Rumors, signs, or suspicions often have some basis in truth — people should investigate what is happening.

Digitalized

The new meaning applies the proverb to relationships — emphasizing that peace of mind is important, but honesty is still necessary. Secrets or deception will eventually be discovered.

Wisdom about truth becomes a warning about cheating.

"Ano man ang gagawin, makapitong iisipin"

Traditional

Before doing anything, a person should think carefully many times. It teaches caution, wisdom, and thoughtful decision-making.

Digitalized

The new meaning applies the proverb to buying or spending — before purchasing something, think carefully about whether it is practical, necessary, or worth the money.

General wisdom becomes practical spending advice.

"Walang nakasisira sa bakal kundi sariling kalawang"

Traditional

Nothing destroys iron except its own rust. A person may be ruined by their own wrong actions, bad habits, or dishonorable choices — or by those closest to them.

Digitalized

The new meaning focuses on inner struggles — highlighting how limiting beliefs, overthinking, and lack of confidence can stop a person from reaching their full potential.

Wrong behavior becomes self-doubt and overthinking.

"May tainga ang lupa, may pakpak ang balita"

Traditional

The earth has ears and news has wings. Information, secrets, or rumors can spread quickly, so people should be careful with what they say or do.

Digitalized

The new meaning applies the proverb to business — if a business has good products and interacts well with people, its reputation can spread quickly through word of mouth.

Gossip travels; so does a good brand reputation.
